WaterMod

Modular water storage

Illustrations and models of low fidelity proottypes

Basic summary of the whole project

For my first year design course, we had to tackle a problem happening around the world. After much research, we decided to try to solve the problem that people in Arroyo Seco, Dominican Republic faced. The main problem not that there wasn't clean water, rather that they lacked a place to store their clean water. They would often put water into dirty vessels, which would introduce contaminants into clean water pouring out of the taps.

Our group went through a iterative design cycle. We first established our design scope. We identified the main users, as well as the needs and the constraints. We built multiple prototypes and kept making improvements. In the end, we chose a modular system which would allow users to freely attach/ detach seperate water storage units with ease.

Intraweb Loading Time Logger

Measures and logs webpage loading time for monitoring purposes

Excel sheet containing data


Source Code



One of the concerns raised by people at the company was that some web-enabled forms would take too long to load. It would also vary based on the time of day that the form was opened at. It would take longer to load during midday, when our traffic was high. I decided to start a project which would log the time every hour, then visualize the data so anyone can see the trends.

I tackled the problem by first testing out bunch of methods - Jenkins and PhantomJS, for example. I switched to I then tried requests library on Python and it worked. After experimenting for a bit, I realized using requests wasn't accurate since it literally just timed how long it took to reach the website and back. I switched to PhantomJS along with Selenium so that the script would accurately measure how long it would take for an average user to load the page on their web browser.

I also used openpyxl, which would log the time it took, as well as the time script was started. The excel sheet would then be connected to Power BI for visualization.

Top Hat Redesign

Redesign to improve usability

Context

Improving education for all

For one of my courses, the only way to access textbook and course contents was through Top Hat. Top Hat is an education platform which professors and students can use to improve their education experience. Unfortunately, that was not the case for us. I decided to see if I can improve the usability of the site and put my skills to test. I have put together a case study for how I created a new redesign for the website.

The Problem

The sites are too inconsistent

I began the process by performing a usability test on some of my classmates. It became evident that those that have never used the platform before had problems with the layout and where to locate things. Additionally, when asked the question, "What are your thoughts on the usability of the site?", most answered that there were too many inconsistencies. For example, one of the three main sections on the site had a totally different layout than the other two. More questions were asked about their likes and dislikes of the product as well.

The Goal

Make things flow

The main goal of the redesign was to make the site consistent. Whether it be layout, colors, or content. User interviews revealed that people liked the Material Design layout for its sleek looks and feel. This also became one of the goals. Last but not least, I felt that some minor changes needed to be introduced to the buttons on the site based on both control and display principles to make it more intuitive.

Market Research

Compare and learn

I picked two sites to compare against - Google Classroom and Brightspace Learning Management System. Google Classroom was chosen due to its material design aspects. Brightspace LMS was chosen since University of Waterloo uses them for classroom content, and students are already familiar with how it works.

Design

Consistent. Material. Intuitive.

An initial sketch was done to design the layout.

The final design hit the goals pretty well. The main requirement, consistancy, was reached through using the same layout as well as color scheme. This was achieved through using Material Design guidelines. Minor changes were made to buttons to make them more salient and intuitive.

GRT EasyGo Redesign<

Redesign of GRT EasyGo app to improve usability

Context

Transit app for KW area

When I tried using the GRT EasyGo app for the first time, I could not help but notice how horrible the app was designed. It was the first item on the app store when I searched "GRT bus" as a newcomer to Waterloo area. As a part of human factors course, we had to do a redesign. My team believed that this app can be improved significantly with some tweaks to its UI. Below is a case study I put together for how the app can be improved. The initial design was done as a group, while the final was done completely by me.

Research

Who are the users and what are the competitions saying?

During this phase, the situation of concern was established. Primary, secondary, and tertiary users were identified. From these, personas were used to identify the potential users of the product. The two personas we identified were: a university student who frequently uses the app, and an older person who occasionally uses the app. These two personas would guide he project in terms of what problems our users would face when using the app.

Personas


Another method used was usability testing. We put a test script together to use on our classmates to gather information about pain areas. The second part of research consisted of market research. Two notable competitors were Google Maps and Transit, which dominate the transit app market today.

Left: Transit, right: Google Maps

The Problem

Safety, performance, preference

With these personas and results from the test in mind, we delve into potential problems that these users may face. The Universal Principles of Design, as well as Nielson's Heuristics were used to come up with areas which the app has problems with. We identified three main problem spaces from research: safety, performance, and preference. These were the focus of the redesign, to make users more safe, make the app faster, and allow users to use integrate their preferences. Fom there, We proceeded to identify the requirements and benchmarks based on the issues found.

The Goal

Make GRT EasyGo Great Again!
